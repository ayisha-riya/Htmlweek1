HTML (HyperText Markup Language)

Invented by Sir Tim Berners-Lee in 1991, HTML was initially a simple tool for scientists at CERN to share research documents across different computers.
Its initial purpose was functional and academic, focusing on creating a system that could link one document to another.
This simple, revolutionary idea gave rise to the World Wide Web,
transforming disparate documents into a connected network of information. Over the decades, HTML has undergone a continuous evolution,
moving from basic text and links to a powerful standard capable of supporting rich multimedia, complex applications, and interactive user experiences.
